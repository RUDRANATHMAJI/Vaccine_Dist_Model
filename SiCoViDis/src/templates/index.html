SIR Model

Modelling the spread of Covid-19

This application is developed to predict the vulnerability of a pandemic for a particular region by using an SIR model. This model analyses the Susceptible, Infected and Recovered population of the region from infection data available to forecast the infection rate. This can lead us to be a proactive force for handling the spread of the infectious diseases like COVID-19, by distributing medical essentials like kits and vaccines. As of April 2021, the urgency of this application has arisen due to the following reasons:

  • As of 14th April, 2020, India has witnessed 20 lakhs daily cases with the second wave just hitting around the corner.
  • The lockdown during the 1st wave of COVID-19 infections in India has caused massive economic losses for the country.
  • The second wave of the infection rates has reached record heights calling for essential efficient models for being prepared as a force to tackle the infection rate.
  • The SIR model gives information about the maximum infection rate and time to reach the maximum infection which helps to take efficient measure to lower the contact rate and to comment on the hospital facilities.

Vaccine Distribution

To cope up with the rapid spreading of COVID-19, we need an efficient model to distribute the vaccine in a vast country like India.
The challenges are both in terms of distances and population. Therefore in this vaccine distribution module, we have developed an optimization technique to model the first stage of vaccine distrbution from the main produsction units to the national centres. The layers of vaccine distribution:

  • From production sites to National Facilities
  • From National Facilities to the state capitals
  • From State Capitals to the District heads
  • From District heads to the Blocks
In the current model, we are trying to find the Facility locations at national level by minimizing the weighted demand of population and demand of each state.
